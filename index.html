Oops! You done goofed

If you're trying to access a docker-osx environment, this most definitely isn't it.

Calm me

/etc/hosts?

docker-osx injects an entry into your /etc/hosts file for locally resolving localdocker. Now is probably a good time to ensure that it's there

A quick grep should look something like this:

$ grep localdocker /etc/hosts
172.16.42.43 localdocker

Correct port?

If you're standing up a local dev environment, odds are good it's listening on a high numbered port for HTTP requests, rather than on plain old port 80. Ensure you are hitting your service on the correct port.

Rails?
Try localdocker:3000.
Sinatra?
How about localdocker:4567?
Django?
Perhaps localdocker:8000 is where it's hiding.

Is anyone listening?

If your containerized service is listening on a loopback address (127.0.0.1), you're gonna have a bad time

With your container up, try running the following command to see whats listening and where:

$ docker-osx ssh -c 'netstat -ltn | grep 127'
